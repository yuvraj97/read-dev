Here we answer binary questions like (if our parameter is \(\theta\) then) \(\theta = 0.5\) or \(\theta \neq 0.5\) using our estimator (say \(\hat{\theta}_n\)).
Now let's look at an example:
How to board a plane(which boarding method is fast):
There are two ways to board a plane:

  1. Rear to front(R2F):
    In this case we start boarding from the rear seats to the front seats.
  2. WilMA(Window,Middle,Aisle):
    In this case we first board the Windows seats, then the Middle seats and then Aisle.
First we collect data for these two methods, say we have data from 2 airlines: airline1(it follows R2F), airlines2(it follows WilMA)
Say that now we only have a summary of data:
R2F WilMA
Average(mins) \(\overline{X}_n=24.2\) \(\overline{Y}_m=15.9\)
Std.Dev(mins) \(\sigma_X=2.1\) \(\sigma_Y=1.3\)
Sample size \(n=72\) \(m=56\)
Model and Assumption
  • \(X\) represents boarding time of airline1(which follows R2F) and \(Y\) represents boarding time of airlines2(which follows WilMA)
  • Assume that \(X\sim\mathcal{N}(\mu_X.\sigma_X^2)\) and \(Y\sim\mathcal{N}(\mu_Y.\sigma_Y^2)\)
    • Here we do allow for \(X\lt 0\) and \(Y\lt 0\) but \(\mathbb{P}(X\lt 0)\) and \(\mathbb{P}(Y\lt 0)\) is negligible.
  • \(X_1,X_2,\cdots,X_n \stackrel{iid}{\sim} X\ ;\quad\) \(Y_1,Y_2,\cdots,Y_m \stackrel{iid}{\sim} Y\) and also two sample are independent (\(X_1,X_2,\cdots,X_n,\)\(Y_1,Y_2,\cdots,Y_m \) are independent)
Now what we want to answer is "are those boarding time are same or one is better then the other":
\(\text{Is } \mu_X=\mu_Y \text{ or }\mu_X \gt \mu_Y \)
But why not answer "\(\text{Is } \mu_X=\mu_Y\)\(\text{ or }\)\(\mu_X \neq \mu_Y \)"
Here we are trying to claim that WilMA is better then R2F, and status quo (\(\mu_X=\mu_Y \)) says before collecting the data there is no reason to believe that one is better then other.
Here we ruling out \(\mu_X\lt\mu_Y \) from our parameter space \((\mathbb{P_\theta})_{\theta\in\Theta}\), so that we can get more out of our data.
A valid scientific inquiry should start from making this assumption (of not allowing \(\mu_X\lt\mu_Y \)) and then collect the data.
Because we can cheat hear by first collecting the data then making the assumption (like we first watch data and see that \(\overline{X}_n\gt\overline{Y}_m\) and based on that we make assumption of not allowing \(\mu_X\lt\mu_Y \)). If we do this we are going to make wrong conclusion.

Here we have two samples one for R2F and another for WilMA, so it's a two sample test, we will see it in next page.
