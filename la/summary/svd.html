Singular Value Decomposition

Say that we have a \(m\times n\) matrix \(A\) and \(\text{Rank}(A)=r\),
Then Singular Value Decomposition of \(A\) is \(A_{m\times n}=U_{m\times m}\Sigma_{m\times n}V_{n\times n}^{T}\).

IDEA: behind SVD is to map an orthonormal basis (say \(v_i\)) in \(\mathbb{R}^n\) (Row space + Null Space of matrix \(A^TA\)) to the orthonormal basis (say \(u_i\)) in \(\mathbb{R}^m\) (Column space + Left Null Space of matrix \(AA^T\)).
\[A\vec{v}_i=\sigma_i\vec{u}_i;\quad\forall i\in\{1,2,\cdots,n\}\] IDEA: to find the \(V\) and \(U\) are the Matrices \(A^TA\) and \(AA^T\)
\(\bullet\)   Row space of \(A\) is same as Row space of \(A^TA\).
\(\Rightarrow\)   Columns of \(V\) are eigenvectors of matrix \(A^TA\)
\(\bullet\)   Column Space of \(A\) is same as Column Space of \(AA^T\)
\(\Rightarrow\)   Columns of \(U\) are eigenvectors of matrix \(AA^T\)
