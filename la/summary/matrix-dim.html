Consider all \(2\times 2\) matrices.
Our matrices looks something like, \(\begin{bmatrix} \color{red}{\mathbb{R}} & \color{yellow}{\mathbb{R}} \\ \color{lime}{\mathbb{R}} & \color{magenta}{\mathbb{R}} \\ \end{bmatrix}\)
\(\bullet\)  We can get any \(2\times 2\) matrix by the linear combination of the Basis of all \(2\times 2\) matrix.

\(\bullet\)  Basis of all \(2\times 2\) matrices: \(\begin{bmatrix} \color{red}{1} & 0 \\ 0 & 0 \\ \end{bmatrix}\) , \(\begin{bmatrix} 0 & \color{red}{1} \\ 0 & 0 \\ \end{bmatrix}\) , \(\begin{bmatrix} 0 & 0 \\ \color{red}{1} & 0 \\ \end{bmatrix}\) , \(\begin{bmatrix} 0 & 0 \\ 0 & \color{red}{1} \\ \end{bmatrix}\)
\(\bullet\)  Dimension of all \(2\times 2\) matrices is \(4\)
