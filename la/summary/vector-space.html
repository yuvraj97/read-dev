A space is a vector space if,

\(\bullet\)  If there is a vector in that space and we multiply that vector with a constant \(c\in\mathbb{R}\) then the resulting vector must be in the space.
\(\bullet\)  Say we took two vectors \(\vec{v}\) and \(\vec{w}\) then there sum must be in that space.
\(\bullet\)  A vector space must pass through the origin.

Say we have two vectors \(\vec{v}, \vec{w} \in \mathbb{R}^d\).
\(\bullet\)  if \(\vec{v}\) is parallel to \(\vec{w}\), then vector space of \(\vec{v}\) and \(\vec{w}\) is a space in \(\mathbb{R}\) (line)
