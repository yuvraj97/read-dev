Kiss example

Recall our Kiss example
\(p\): Proportion of couples who turns their head to the right.
\(n\): Total number of observation
According to study \(n=124\) and \(80\) peoples turns their head to the right.
Here we are testing "do people have any preference to turn their head to left or right?"
\(H_0: p=1/2; \quad H_1: p \neq 1/2\)
It's Two sided test
Modelling Assumptions
We have \(n\) observations.
Say we have \(n\) i.i.d. r.v. with Bernoulli distribution.
\(X_1,...,X_n\stackrel{iid}{\sim}\text{Ber}(p);\quad p\in(0,1)\)
\[X_i=\left\{\begin{matrix} 1\quad\text{;if ith person turns to right}\quad\quad\quad \\ \ 0\quad\text{;if ith person did not turns to right} \end{matrix}\right.\]
Estimator
Estimator of \(p\):
\(\hat{p}_n = \frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^nX_i =\overline{X}_n\)
\[\hat{p}_n = \frac{80}{124}=64.5\%\]
Test statistics
\(H_0: p=1/2; \quad H_1: p \neq 1/2\)
So we want to test the deviation of our estimate \(\hat{p}_n\) from true parameter \(p\)
We can get the deviation as, \(|\hat{p}_n-p|\), and then we can say if \(|\hat{p}_n-p|\) is far away from \(0\), then according to our data, true parameter is unlikely to be \(p\)
\(\mathbb{E}[\hat{p}_n-p] = 0,\quad\) \(\text{var}(\hat{p}_n-p) = \text{var}(\hat{p}_n) = \frac{p(1-p)}{n}\)
Then by CLT \[\sqrt{n}\frac{\hat{p}_n-p}{\sqrt{p(1-p)}} \xrightarrow [n\to \infty ]{(d)} \mathcal{N}(0,1)\] We will reject the null hypothesis \(H_0\) if,
\(|\hat{p}_n-p|\gt C;\quad C\in\mathbb{R}^+\)
\(\Rightarrow \sqrt{n}\frac{|\hat{p}_n-p|}{\sqrt{p(1-p)}} \gt \sqrt{n}\frac{C}{\sqrt{p(1-p)}} \)

\(\Rightarrow \sqrt{n}\frac{|\hat{p}_n-p|}{\sqrt{p(1-p)}} \gt C';\quad C\in\mathbb{R}^+\)
So we define our test statistics to be,
\[T_n = \sqrt{n}\frac{|\hat{p}_n-p|}{\sqrt{p(1-p)}} \]
Test
\[\psi =𝟙\{T_n\gt C\};\quad C\in\mathbb{R}^+\]
\(\text{p-value}\)
First we assume that our null hypothesis is true, so \(p=1/2\)
\[\Rightarrow T_n = \sqrt{124}\frac{|0.645-0.5|}{\sqrt{0.5(1-0.5)}}=3.2293\] \(\text{p-value}=\mathbb{P}_{\theta\in\Theta_0}\left( |Z| \gt T_n \right),\) and \(Z\sim \mathcal{N}(0,1)\)
\(\text{p-value}=\mathbb{P}_{p\in\{1/2\}}\left( |Z| \gt T_n \right)\)
\(\text{p-value}=\mathbb{P}\left( |Z| \gt 3.2293 \right)\)
\(\text{p-value}=2\left( 1 - \mathbb{P}\left( Z \lt 3.2293 \right) \right)\)
\(\text{p-value}\approx 2\left( 1 - 0.99938 \right)\)
\(\text{p-value}\approx 0.00124\)
This \(\text{p-value}\) is way small so we have a very strong evidence against the null hypothesis.
Now if we choose our threshold to be \(0.01\) or say \(1\%\).
Then \(\text{p-value}\lt 0.01\), so we reject the null hypothesis.
