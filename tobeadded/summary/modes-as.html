Almost surely \((\text{a.s.})\) convergence

A sequence of random variables \(X_1,X_2,X_3,\cdots\) is defined on a sample space \(S\). Here each \(X_n\) is a function that maps \(S\) to \(\mathbb{R}\) and this sequence converges almost surely to a random variable \(X\) if: \[\mathbb{P}\left( \left\{s \in S: X_n(s) \xrightarrow [n\to \infty ]{} X(s)\right\}\right)=1\]
