Convergence in probability

A sequence of random variables \(X_1,X_2,X_3,\cdots\) converges to a random variable \(X\):
\(X_n \xrightarrow [n\to \infty ]{\mathbb{P}} X\quad\) iff: \[\mathbb{P}\left[ \left| X_n - X \right| \geq \varepsilon \right] \xrightarrow [n\to \infty ]{} 0,\quad\forall\varepsilon \gt 0 \] Almost surely \((\text{a.s.})\) convergence \(\Rightarrow\) Convergence in probability
