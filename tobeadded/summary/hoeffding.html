Say that we have \(n\) i.i.d. random variables, \(X_1,X_2,....,X_n \stackrel{iid}{\sim } X\), such that \(\mathbb{E}[X]=\mu\) and
\(X \in [a,b]\) almost surely

▶ Hoeffding's inequality \[\mathbb{P}\left(\left|\overline{X}_n -\mathbb{E}[X]\right| \geq \epsilon \right) \leq 2 \exp\left(-\frac{2n\epsilon ^2}{(b-a)^2}\right)\quad \forall\epsilon >0\]
