Here our Goal is to get an estimate of probability distribution of our data.
What we have seen so far (regarding estimation),
  • A real life situation and some questions.
    • First have a real life situation and we want to answer some questions.
    Like in our Coin Toss Example we are tossing a coin multiple time and the question we want are asking is "is this coin fair or not?"
  • Build a Statistical Model
    • To answer these questions we build a statistical model \(\left(E,(\mathbb{P_\theta})_{\theta\in\Theta}\right)\)
    \(E\): Sample space
    \((\mathbb{P_\theta})_{\theta\in\Theta}\): it's family of probability distribution on \(E\)
    \(\Theta\): Parameter set
    Like in our Coin Toss Example the statistical model is \[\left( \{0,1\}, (Ber(p))_{p\in(0,1)} \right)\] here we parameterized the "probability of flipping a head" as \(p\)
  • Estimate the parameter
    • Now we had out Statistical Model now we want to estimate our parameter.
    We used average as our estimation technique, it is justified by the Law of Large numbers.
    Once we find the parameter (and if our parameter if identifiable) then we can get it's distribution.
  • Confidence Interval for our estimate
    • After getting our estimate we find a range with some confidence, our estimator is in this range.
    Central limit theorem helps us to find that range, and what CLT do is it give us an estimate of distribution of our data.
    Once we have an estimate of our parameter (and if our parameter if identifiable) then we can get it's distribution.
    But there might be some distortion when we estimate whole probability distribution of our i.i.d. data by that parameter estimate.
    Here in Methods of Estimation we will see various methods to estimate a probability distribution.
